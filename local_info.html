Location

The school and workshop will be held at the University of Tasmania's Sandy Bay campus. Sandy Bay is an approximately 5 minute drive from the centre of the city of Hobart. Travel down Sandy Bay Road, turn right into the University, we are at the 4th building on the right hand side (see map above).

Local attractions

There are many cultural and tourism attractions close to the conference venue.

SALAMANCA MARKET
Salamanca market runs every Saturday from around 8:30am until around 3pm. It is set in the historial Salamanca precinct right next to the Hobart waterfront. Featuring more than 300 stalls of local craftwork, music, food and art, it's the perfect place for souvenirs and a fantastic breakfast.

MUSEUM OF OLD AND NEW ART
Australia's largest private art collection, MONA has almost single-handedly put Hobart on the global cultural map.
KUNANYI (MOUNT WELLINGTON)
Mount Wellington, or kunanyi (local Aboriginal name), is an unmissable part of the Hobart skyline. The summit is accessible by car.
